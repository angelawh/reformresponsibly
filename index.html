What is responsible reform?

Conversation

We must come together as a community of undergraduates and faculty, despite our different opinions, to develop academic policies that reflect our values.

Time

Adminsitrative bureaucracy is bad, but so is making sweeping changes overnight. We have a concrete plan to capture all voices in the conversation in the spring semester.

Accountability

The Honor Code is valuable because it requires us to be accountable to our peers rather than policing professors. We must protect the Code from detrimental changes.

Critiques on the Referenda

Referendum 1

The Referendum Text

Shall the Honor Constitution be amended to (1) change the standard penalty for a first violation of the Honor Code from a one-year suspension to disciplinary probation, (2) change the standard penalty for a second violation of the Honor Code from expulsion to a one-year suspension, and (3) stipulate that a violation of the Honor Code involving “extenuating circumstances” will result in a finding of not responsible, instead of disciplinary probation?

What we want you to know

Reduced standard penalty translates to standard leniency. We recognize the need for gradated penalty, so the sanction matches the severity of the violation; however, we do not think the solution is to lower penalty across the board. A student who writes 45-seconds overtime should not be penalized as severely as the student who Googles solutions on thier cellular phone.

Referendum 2

The Referendum Text

Shall Article III, Section C, Subsection 6 of the Honor Constitution be amended to add that an “[alleged Honor Code violation] case brought to hearing must have at least two separate pieces of evidence, each of which indicates that a violation occurred”?

What We Want You to Know

The Committee requires two pieces of evidence that a violation may have occurred to move a case to hearing. Codifying this norm is a great idea, but language matters. The word "indicates" forces the Chair to say whether a vioaltion occurred before a student ever has a chance to argue their case before the Committee.

Referendum 3

The Referendum Text

Shall Article II, Section D of the Honor Constitution be amended to add that “a student will be found not responsible [of an Honor Code violation] if the Committee fails to find overwhelmingly convincing evidence that the student ought reasonably to have understood that their actions were in violation of the Honor Code, or if the course instructor explicitly states that a student’s actions were not in violation of their class policy"?

What we want you to know

As students, we are unified by our affiliation to the University and our acceptance of the Code upon matriculation. The same is not true for professors and preceptors, who may have been educated at different undergraduate institutions and have different notions of what constitutes academic integrity. Allowing a professor to determine a student's outcome at hearing undermines a system for student-to-student accountability.
